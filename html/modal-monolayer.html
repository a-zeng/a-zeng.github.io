Automated Marangoni Monolayer Technique

A machine that dispenses a solution of particles onto water, densifies them into a single layer sheet, then transfers the sheet (monolayer) onto a substrate

This is the culmination of all the stuff that I've learned from Northeastern and during co-op: my senior capstone project. Our team was a group of five, and we were tasked to automate the process of creating a large, densified monolayer for one of our professors.

Full Render of our System

Objectives:
  • Cover a 9in-by-9in substrate with a hexagonal Boron Nitride (hBN) monolayer
  • Condense the monolayer using the Marangoni effect
Biggest Challenges:
  • Stopping hBN in the hBN-ethanol solution from sedimenting in the Erlenmeyer flask and in the dispensing tubes
  • Preventing contamination that would reduce the surface tension of the water in the trough before the Marangoni blade induced the Marangoni flow
  • Discovering that the substrate has to be angled with respect to the water so that instabilities when the substrate is pulled from the water do not destroy the monolayer
Results:
  • 1st place in our capstone track
  • Successfully coats the desired substrate with hBN
  • Intuitive user interface for easy control of the system
  • Rigid and sturdy frame
  • Adjustable substrate using bubble levels
Takeaways:
  • Fail fast and fail hard
  • Create your prototype as soon as possible to maximize testing
  • A tested minimum viable product is far better than a fully-featured, half-baked product
