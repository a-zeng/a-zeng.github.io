Watney MK3

A Mars rover made by Northeastern students for competition in the University Rover Challenge (URC)

The Northeastern Mars Rover Team (NUROVER) was founded in September 2018, originally with the intention of competing in the following year's Mars Society University Rover Challenge (URC). The challenge requires the rover to partake in tasks emulating Mars' desert-like terrain, such as manipulating tools, interacting with objects, and autonomously navigating rocky terrain, all under a $18,000 budget and 50kg weight limit. More information can be found at the Mars Society's website here. Our website can be found here.

2021-2022 Season - Watney MK3

Taking on a role as the arm lead for this year (as well as co-leading the mechanical team), my goal was ambitious: spearhead the development of a custom robotic arm within a 8 month period capable of moving dextrously around its workspace (on top of the rover!) and manipulating heavy objects.

Arm Goals:
  • Has 6 degrees of freedom (DoF), excluding a gripper
  • Capable of lifting 5kg at 1m away from the arm's base
  • Has closed-feedback sensing on each joint
  • Weigh less than 10kg
  • Cost less than $4000
Challenges:
  • Training new members while also leading the development effort of the arm
  • Designing an arm capable of fulfilling requirements when industrial robotic arms of the same capabilities cost significantly more
  • Determining anticipated loads per joint of the arm
  • Procuring parts for the arm from the most optimal vendors
  • Estimating cost and weight of the arm prior to assembly
  • Creating a custom arm control solution (joint-replica model) that is more intuitive for users
Results:
  • 6 DoF arm with joint sensing
  • Cost $3,406.92
  • Weighs ~9kg
  • Can lift 6kg at 1m and manipulate objects
  • Controllable via haptic arm (joint-replica model)
  • 16th place of 36 teams during competition as a team that has never gone to Utah
